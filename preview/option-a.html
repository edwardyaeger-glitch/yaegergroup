About

The Yaeger Group began in 2018 as a coaching practice. The early work grew by referral among people who could have called anyone, in fields where that means something. They kept coming back, and they kept sending others.

By 2020, many of the conversations had become executive consulting in everything but name. They were no longer about a single person's career; they were about how a person was carrying an organization, or how an organization was failing the person inside it. The work was named what it had already become.

Clinical psychotherapy followed, deliberately and the slow way: graduate school, practicum, internship, three thousand supervised hours, board licensure. It was a midlife addition, not a credential picked up in passing, and it was added because the work kept arriving at a layer that coaching and consulting alone were not built to meet.

A midlife addition, not a credential picked up in passing.

Each discipline, on its own, has a ceiling. Coaching, which became advising, can name what someone wants but not always what is holding them back. Consulting can name what an organization needs but not always what its people can carry. Therapy can meet the inner life but not the structures shaping it. Held together, each is sharper. The practice has been built around that observation since.

I  ·  Consulting

A consultant who understands clinical psychology knows why the conversation is stuck, not just that it is.

II  ·  Advisory

An advisor with executive experience doesn't just map a next move, but accounts for what it will actually demand.

III  ·  Psychotherapy

A therapist who has sat in boardrooms doesn't treat stress in the abstract, but in the specific context that created it.

The engagements themselves stay separate - therapy is therapy, advisory is advisory, consulting is consulting - each held to its own standard and confidentiality. What carries across is the thinking behind them.

About Edward

Edward J. Yaeger Jr.

Over twenty-five years of experience across business, consulting, academia, and clinical practice.

I am a first-generation college graduate from a working class family in the City of Philadelphia. Over twenty-five years, I have worked across business, consulting, academia, and clinical practice, advising leaders, public figures, and professionals whose decisions carry real consequence. My clinical licensure came later in life, after three prior degrees and years of advisory work, because the need for it became essential.

Long ago, I was a competitive athlete: a nationally-ranked sprint triathlete, competitive water polo player, and All-American swimmer, having trained under Philadelphia Department of Recreation Swimming Hall-of-Famer, Coach Jim Ellis, on whom the feature-length film, Pride, is based. This was right around the time I was bopping about errant modeling gigs for popular magazines like GQ and consumer fashion brands like J.Crew.

I have frequently contributed op-ed pieces to major publications, such as HuffPost and The Advocate, lectured at academic centers across North America and Europe, including Johns Hopkins, UBC, and Bocconi, and guest paneled at events hosted by major professional associations and advocacy groups, including ACA, AMA, and HRC.

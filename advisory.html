ii.  Advisory

Professional and vocational strategy grounded in clinical and executive experience.

Advisory is structured, goal-oriented work focused on career, vocation, and professional direction. It draws on the same relational depth as clinical work, but the scope is different: working through a challenge, building toward a specific outcome, or pressure-testing a decision. Engagements can be session-based, project-based, or a defined series.

Vocational Advisory

We work with individuals whose professional lives are highly visible, tightly managed, and consequential: artists, performers, leaders, and public figures whose decisions ripple outward to teams, organizations, and audiences. At that level, many familiar forms of guidance stop being useful. Not because they are incompetent, but because they are structurally unsuited to the realities involved.

  • Public figures: navigating visibility, scrutiny, and the gap between persona and self
  • Senior leaders: weighing succession, legacy, or a consequential next move
  • Artists and performers: managing the relationship between identity, output, and audience
  • Founders and principals: carrying decisions whose weight cannot be shared inside the organization

Our vocational advisory work draws on clinical psychotherapy, executive consulting, and years of business experience. Not as a bundle of services. As an approach. The focus is coherence: between values and action; identity and responsibility; ambition and meaning.

It is structured, reflective work intended to support sound judgment and sustained orientation under pressure.

We work privately and almost entirely by referral. The work is individualized, discreet, and not designed to scale. It exists for moments when the cost of continuing as things are - quiet, cumulative, and often unnamed - has begun to outweigh the discomfort of addressing what has shifted.

Contract Advisory

Organizations often need sophisticated support for leaders-in-training that neither executive coaching nor traditional therapy can provide alone. Because we are both licensed clinicians and advisors with deep experience in business, employees receive coherent, integrated support.

  • High-potential employees: preparing for leadership roles
  • Managers: working through authority, visibility, or influence
  • Employees under strain: during promotion, role expansion, or organizational change
  • Retention investments: meaningful support for valued talent through periods of transition and growth

Engagements are typically short- to medium-term retainers focused on individual development while remaining aligned with organizational goals and governance. Conversations begin by clarifying scope, goals, governance, and fit.

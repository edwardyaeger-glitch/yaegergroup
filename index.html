Independent judgment for consequential moments

The Work

i.

Independent judgment for leaders and organizations navigating high-stakes moments.

There are moments when something important is not working, yet nothing is ostensibly “broken.” Decisions drag. Conversations repeat. People perform competence while carefully avoiding what actually needs to be addressed. The organization keeps moving, but judgment erodes and trust ebbs. Everyone feels it, but no one quite names it, primarily out of fear.

For most people in an organization, guidance exists. Managers turn to senior leaders, who draw on boards, consultancies, and counsel. In many situations, that ecosystem is sufficient. But when the issue is personal, politically sensitive, or psychologically complex, it often is not. That is where I come in.

I help surface what is interfering with judgment and support leaders in dealing with it before the cost compounds further.

When the issue is not strategy, legality, optics, or execution, but how power is being exercised, how responsibility is being carried, or how avoidance has quietly taken hold, traditional guidance often stops short. Not because it is wrong, but because it is not designed for that layer of reality.

I work privately and almost entirely by referral. The work is not standardized, not public-facing, and not designed to scale; it involves direct conversations about judgment, consequence, and responsibility, handled carefully, without sycophancy, flattery, or agenda.

I am brought in to provide independent judgment, clarity, and composure when existing structures are no longer sufficient to do so.

Executive-Level Consulting

Most management consultancies focus on strategy, technology, scale, and efficiency. Even those grounded in industrial/organizational psychology have historically deprioritized the human dimension, as revenue models tended to favor long-term retainers and incremental upselling.

In the post-Big Data era, and especially with the rise of AI, the true competitive differentiator will be how organizations understand, support, and engage their own people. Judgment, creativity, and meaning-making cannot be automated.

Consulting Focus

I work with executive teams and senior leaders on organizational culture, leadership behavior, identity and role complexity, and the human response to disruption. This work prioritizes clarity, containment, and sustainability. Not performative culture initiatives, baseless trends, or abstract frameworks.

This is not HR consulting. It is executive-level work on how people actually think, feel, relate, and perform inside complex organizations.

ii.

Career strategy grounded in clinical and executive experience.

Advisory is a structured, goal-oriented engagement focused on career, vocation, and professional direction. It draws on the same relational depth as clinical work, but the scope is different. We are not treating a diagnosis. We are working through a challenge, building toward a specific outcome, or pressure-testing a decision.

Who Advisory Is For

Advisory is appropriate for professionals at any stage who are working through questions that are fundamentally about their professional direction, not their mental health: navigating transitions, managing the demands of leadership or ownership, or recalibrating after a setback.

How It Works

Engagements begin with an initial conversation to define objectives and determine fit. Advisory can be session-based, project-based, or a defined series. Fees are discussed individually.

Vocational Advisory

There are moments when someone's professional life appears coherent, successful, and fully resourced, yet something essential begins to fray beneath the surface. The work continues. The decisions keep coming. The public-facing machine functions. And yet judgment dulls, motivation wanes, and choices that once felt intuitive become effortful. Nothing is ostensibly “broken,” but something important is no longer working.

I work with individuals whose professional lives are highly visible, tightly managed, and consequential: artists, performers, leaders, and public figures whose decisions ripple outward to teams, organizations, and audiences. At that level, many familiar forms of guidance stop being useful. Not because they are incompetent, but because they are structurally unsuited to the realities involved.

Keep the rigor of a therapist. Remove the emotional bias of loved ones. Account for the structural incentives that shape agency relationships. What remains is the need for an independent, trusted advisor.

I am not an extension of a team. I am not accountable to anyone's internal (or external) hierarchy. I am engaged precisely because I am not embedded in any of it.

I do not manage logistics, messaging, or performance. I work independently, and only when independence is essential. Both yours and mine.

My vocational advisory work draws on licensed psychotherapy, professional counseling, and decades of advisory experience, not as a bundle of services, but as a way of working. The focus is not motivation or optimization, but coherence: between values and action; identity and responsibility; ambition and meaning.

This is not management coaching or motivational advising. It is structured, reflective work intended to support sound judgment and sustained orientation under pressure.

I work privately and almost entirely by referral. The work is individualized, discreet, and not designed to scale. It exists for moments when the cost of continuing as things are - quiet, cumulative, and often unnamed - has begun to outweigh the discomfort of addressing what has shifted.

Contract Advisory

Organizations often identify leaders-in-training early but lack sophisticated support structures to help those individuals evolve from effective managers into adaptive, reflective leaders.

Traditional executive coaches can offer mentorship and strategy but cannot ethically provide psychotherapy. Most therapists, even through EAPs, lack the organizational fluency to support leadership development and career complexity.

Because I am both a licensed clinician and a trusted advisor with over twenty-five years of experience in business, employees receive coherent, integrated support rather than fragmented services across coaching techniques, therapy interventions, and HR policies. This engagement bridges leadership development and professional identity; emotional intelligence and psychological resilience; career planning and decision-making; and stress management and mental health support.

  • High-potential employees: preparing for leadership roles
  • Managers: working through authority, visibility, or influence
  • Employees under strain: during promotion, role expansion, or organizational change
  • Retention investments: meaningful support for valued talent through periods of transition and growth

Engagements are typically short- to medium-term retainers focused on individual development while remaining aligned with organizational goals and governance. Conversations begin by clarifying scope, goals, governance, and fit.

iii.

Private, long-term clinical work for professionals in demanding environments.

Psychotherapy requires more than education, training, and experience. It requires judgment, restraint, and the capacity to tolerate complexity without rushing to resolution.

Much of what brings people into therapy is not immediately dramatic or diagnosable. It is quieter: a sense that familiar ways of coping no longer hold; that decisions feel heavier than they should; that roles once inhabited with ease now require effort or performance.

The aim is not to optimize or perform wellness, but to understand what is happening, why it is happening, and how to respond in a way that can be sustained and lived with over time.

My clinical work is grounded in licensed training and conducted within ethical and professional boundaries. I am attentive to role clarity and do not blur psychotherapy with advisory, consulting, or performance-oriented work. What differentiates the work is not a particular technique or orientation, but the way responsibility is held: I attend closely to context, personal, relational, cultural, and professional, without collapsing the work into problem-solving or reassurance.

I take an interpersonal, interactive, integrative, and eclectic approach, drawing from an array of applied theories, modalities, and schools of thought rather than from one singular orientation.

I work deliberately and selectively. Not every concern requires therapy, and not every therapeutic approach is a good fit.

Yaeger Group

About

The Yaeger Group provides executive consulting, vocational advisory, and clinical psychotherapy. Few practices offer all three. Fewer still integrate them. Each discipline is sharper because it draws on the other two.

A consultant who understands clinical psychology knows why the room is stuck, not just that it is.

An advisor with executive experience doesn't just map a next move, but accounts for what it will actually demand.

A therapist who has sat in boardrooms doesn't treat stress in the abstract, but in the specific context that created it.

This work is more precise because it draws on more than one lens. Problems get named faster, decisions get made with more context, and support does not stop where one discipline's vocabulary ends. This is what the Yaeger Group makes possible. And the possibilities have been profound.
